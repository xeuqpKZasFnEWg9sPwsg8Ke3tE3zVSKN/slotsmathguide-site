Wagering requirements, sometimes called playthrough or rollover, are bonus terms that tell you how much you need to wager before you can withdraw bonus funds and, in many cases, winnings tied to that bonus.

Because every operator and promotion can define these terms differently, the details always come down to the specific offer’s rules. Still, the underlying idea is usually straightforward once you know what to look for.

What is a wagering requirement?

A wagering requirement is a condition attached to a bonus (or free spins) that requires you to place a certain total amount of bets on qualifying games.

It is usually expressed as a multiplier, like 20x, 30x, or 40x.

The basic math (with a simple example)

A common structure is:

Required wagering = bonus amount × wagering multiplier

Illustrative example:

  • Bonus: $100
  • Wagering: 30x
  • Required wagering: $100 × 30 = $3,000

This $3,000 refers to the total amount you place as bets, not the amount you need to win or lose.

Does the multiplier apply to the bonus only, or deposit + bonus?

This is one of the biggest “read the terms” items.

  • Some offers apply wagering to the **bonus only**.
  • Others apply it to the **deposit + bonus**.
  • Some promotions also treat **winnings from free spins** differently.

If the terms do not clearly say what the multiplier applies to, treat it as a red flag and contact the operator’s support before opting in.

What typically counts toward wagering (and what may not)

Wagering requirements nearly always have rules about what activity qualifies.

Common examples (these vary by offer):

  • Some games may contribute 100% toward wagering, while others contribute less, or may not count at all.
  • Some promotions require you to wager with real money first, or separate deposit funds from bonus funds.
  • Some require bets to be within certain limits, like a minimum bet size and a maximum bet size.

A safe mindset: assume that not every bet will necessarily count the same way, then confirm the specifics.

Game contribution (weighting) explained

“Contribution” means only a percentage of your wager counts toward the requirement.

Illustrative example:

  • You need $3,000 in wagering.
  • Slots contribute 100%.
  • A particular game contributes 10%.

If you wager $10 on the 10% game, it only adds $1 toward the $3,000 requirement.

Why this matters: if most of your play is on low-contribution games, it can take much longer (and cost more) to complete the requirement.

Time limits, max bet rules, and max cashout caps

Many promotions include extra conditions beyond the multiplier.

Time limits

Some offers require you to complete wagering within a certain number of days. If you miss the deadline, the bonus and any bonus-linked winnings may be removed.

Maximum bet size

Some bonus terms set a maximum allowed stake per spin/hand while the bonus is active. Going over that limit can sometimes void the promotion.

Maximum cashout (capped winnings)

Some bonuses cap how much you can withdraw from bonus-derived winnings. If a cap exists, it can change the practical value of the bonus.

“Sticky” vs “non-sticky” bonuses (not universal)

You may see terms like:

  • **Sticky bonus:** the bonus funds are removed first when you withdraw.
  • **Non-sticky bonus:** you may be able to withdraw winnings first.

These labels are not used consistently everywhere, so focus on the operator’s actual description of what happens when you withdraw while a bonus is active.

Common wagering requirement gotchas

Here are issues that commonly cause confusion:

  • Excluded games or reduced contribution for certain game types.
  • Different rules for bonus funds vs deposit funds.
  • Bet size limits that can void the offer.
  • A time limit you did not notice.
  • A max cashout cap that limits what you can withdraw from bonus-derived winnings.
  • Confusing wagering requirements with identity checks (KYC). Verification requirements are separate from wagering.

A quick checklist before you claim a bonus

Before you opt in, check:

1. The wagering multiplier and what it applies to (bonus only vs deposit+bonus).

2. Which games contribute, and at what percentages.

3. Any time limit to complete wagering.

4. Any maximum bet rules while the bonus is active.

5. Any max cashout/capped winnings rule.

FAQ

Are wagering requirements always the same?

No. Even if two offers show the same multiplier, other terms like game contribution, time limit, maximum bet, and max cashout can change how hard the requirement is to complete.

Do all games count 100%?

Not necessarily. Many promotions assign different contribution rates to different games, and some may not count at all.

Can I withdraw right away after claiming a bonus?

Often, you cannot withdraw bonus funds (and sometimes bonus-linked winnings) until you meet the promotion’s rules. The exact behavior depends on the offer.
