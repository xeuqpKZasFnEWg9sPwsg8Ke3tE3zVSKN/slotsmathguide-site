Wagering requirements, explained (with simple math and real examples).

TL;DR: Required betting = multiplier × base, then adjust for contribution. The base is usually bonus only or deposit + bonus.

Wagering (also called playthrough, rollover, or turnover) is the total amount you must bet before bonus funds, and often bonus-linked winnings, become withdrawable. Most promos are decided by three lines: what’s multiplied, what counts, and what voids wins. Find those lines and you can quickly tell whether a “30x” bonus is reasonable or a withdrawal trap.

Two examples that explain most promos

If you only learn one thing: the base matters. The same “30x” can mean double the required betting.

Example A (bonus-only wagering)
$100 bonus, 30x bonus → $3,000 wagering
You must place $3,000 in total bets before bonus funds (and often bonus-linked winnings) unlock for withdrawal, assuming you follow the other rules.
Example B (deposit + bonus wagering)
$100 deposit + $100 bonus, 30x → $6,000 wagering
Same multiplier, double the base. This is often the hidden reason a promo feels “impossible”.

Red flags that cause “can’t withdraw” (and what to do)
  • Deposit+bonus wagering: the base is bigger than it looks.
  • Low contribution: the game you play may count 0–20% (or not at all).
  • Max bet: exceed it and winnings can be voided.
  • Restricted games: some slots, tables, or live games are excluded.
  • Time limit: 7–30 days is common, sometimes less.
  • Max cashout: caps your withdrawal even if you win more.

What counts as wagering?

Usually total bets placed on eligible games. Some sites exclude bonus rounds, feature buys, or specific providers.

Why “max bet” is serious

Max bet rules can void winnings if you exceed a per-spin cap while on bonus. It’s one of the most common reasons withdrawals get rejected.

Step 1: Find what the multiplier applies to

Look for wording like “bonus amount” vs “deposit + bonus” (or “deposit and bonus”). If it’s deposit+bonus, your wagering target is immediately higher.

What to screenshot
  • The exact wagering line (multiplier + base).
  • Any max bet / max cashout line.
  • Contribution / restricted games table if present.
Step 2: Check contribution rates (what counts)

If your game contributes 10%, you effectively need 10× the total bets to clear the same target. This is the most common hidden “gotcha”.

Quick example

You have $3,000 wagering to clear. If the game counts 10%, you’ll need about $30,000 in total bets to clear it.

Conversion tip

Before you opt in, look for a table that says “Slots 100% / Table games 0–20%” or a list of excluded games. If you can’t find it, assume tables and live games contribute little or nothing.

FAQ
What does “wagering 30x” actually mean?

It means your total bets must reach 30 × the base. The base might be the bonus amount, or it might be deposit + bonus. That one detail can double your requirement before contribution is even considered.

What does “playthrough” mean?

Playthrough is another word for wagering requirements. Some casinos use “rollover” or “turnover”. The math is the same: multiplier × base, then adjust for contribution.

Do winnings have wagering too?

Often yes when you accept a bonus, but the exact rule varies by promo. Check whether the requirement applies to “bonus funds”, “bonus funds and winnings”, or “bonus + deposit”, and watch for caps like max cashout.

Why do some games count less toward wagering?

Casinos assign contribution rates (0–100%). If a game contributes 10%, you effectively need about 10× more total bets to clear the same target. Tables and live games are commonly low or excluded.

What’s the biggest red flag for withdrawals?

Deposit+bonus wagering combined with low contribution or a strict max bet. Add a low max cashout and you have the classic “why can’t I withdraw?” scenario.

How do I read max bet rules?

Look for a line like “Maximum bet while bonus is active: $X per spin (or per round).” Treat it as a hard rule. If you exceed it, casinos may void winnings, not just reduce them.

Is it ever worth taking a high-wagering bonus?

Sometimes, but treat it as entertainment unless the terms are unusually clean: bonus-only wagering, high contribution on the games you actually play, a reasonable max bet, and no harsh max cashout. If you care about cashing out, cross-check the withdrawal basics in withdrawal times.
